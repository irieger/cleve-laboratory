Melancholia Detail.

Albrecht's etching Melancholia can be seen in MATLAB with the commands

   load durer
   image(X)
   colormap(map)
   axis image
   axis off

Among the many mathematical objects on display is a 4-by-4 matrix in the upper right hand corner. The matrix is a magic square -- all the rows and columns, as well as both principle diagonals have the same sum.

You can see the magic square more closely with

   load detail
   image(X)
   colormap(map)
   axis image
   axis off

MATLAB has a function that generates magic squares of any size (greater than 2). The magic square in Durer's etching and one generated by the MATLAB function differ slightly.

   M = magic(4)

M =
    16     2     3    13
     5    11    10     8
     9     7     6    12
     4    14    15     1

The one in the etching has the date that the etching was created in the center of the last row.

In this app we made it possible to permute rows or columns by clicking on any two of them.

  • Is it possible to reach this magic square with the date in the right-hand column?
